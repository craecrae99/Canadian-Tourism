5 Places to Visit in Canada

The Rideau Canal

 

The Rideau Canal is a very popular attraction during the winter. It was made in Ottawa to transport ships throughout Southern Ontario and nowadys during the winter it's a popular attraction for people to skate on it as it's the largest ice rink in the world. It cuts the city almost in half and it is nice just to walk along during the summer, but if you decide to go during the winter I would recommend you bring some warm clothing as it can be really cold.

 

Kluane National Park

 

Kluane National Park is a beautiful park in the northern Canadian territory, Yukon. Yukon is in the North-West of the country. As Yukon is in the North most people travel there in the summer to enjoy the warm weather and the hiking for the incredible views. If you were to go in the winter it would be very hard to hike around and the views would not be as good because of the layer of snow.

 

Banff National Park

 

Banff National Park is another one of the many beautiful parks in Canada. Two notable locations in Banff are Moraine lake(pictured on the far left.) and Johnston Canyon(Pictured on the close left.). Banff was also the original Canadian National park being established in 1885 with the Rockies mountain range running right through it is is a beautiful sight.

 

Toronto

 

Toronto is the most populated city in Canada and is a very popular travel destination because of city, all the sports, the CN Tower, and many other reasons. One of the other reasons people visit their is because they love art, Toronto has one of the most iconic skylines and well as many highly acclaimed museums.

 

The Maritimes

 

The Maritime are made up of 3 provinces Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, Prince Edward Island(P.E.I for short). There is a really interesting, mostly rocky coastline there, one of the attractions in the Maritimes is Peggy's cove, it is known for having 50 foot(15 metre) waves(Pictured far left). Another attraction in the Maritimes is the magnetic hills where gravity is almost reversed.
